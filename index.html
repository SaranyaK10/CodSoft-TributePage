Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Born October 15 1931, Rameswaram, India and died July 27, 2015, Shillong was an Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was president of India from 2002 to 2007. His scientific achievements and popularity gained him the epithets “Missile Man” and “People’s President.”

Career

Kalam was born in Tamil Nadu into a family of a fishing boat owner. Despite financial difficulties, he pursued education in aeronautical engineering. He joined DRDO in 1958 and later moved to ISRO, where he was the project director of SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle. Under his guidance, India successfully launched the Rohini satellite in 1980.

Achievements

In 1969, Dr. Kalam was transferred to ISRO, where he played a key role in India’s first SLV-III project. Under his leadership, India successfully launched the Rohini satellite in 1980, marking its entry into the space age. He also contributed to the development of missile technology and worked with renowned scientists like Dr. Vikram Sarabhai and Dr. Satish Dhawan.

History & Legacy

Despite financial struggles, Dr. Kalam pursued his dreams in aerospace engineering. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture to students, leaving behind a legacy of inspiration. His birthday is celebrated as World Students' Day in India.

Kalam wrote several books, including an autobiography, Wings of Fire (1999), and a book titled Ignited Minds (2002). Among his numerous awards were three of the country’s highest honors: the Padma Bhushan (1981), the Padma Vibhushan (1990), and the Bharat Ratna (1997).
